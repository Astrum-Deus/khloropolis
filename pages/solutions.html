The Problems

The Concrete Jungle Effect

Rapid urbanization in cities like Yangon has replaced natural soil and trees with concrete and asphalt. These surfaces absorb and trap heat, dramatically raising city temperatures compared to rural surroundings, making summers increasingly severe.

Limited Urban Space

With high-density residential buildings and narrow streets, finding ground space for traditional parks or large gardens is nearly impossible. Without creative alternatives, city residents remain cut off from nature and fresh air.

Air Quality & Health

Fewer trees mean less natural filtration for vehicle emissions and dust. This lack of urban greenery leads to poor air quality, rising respiratory issues, and a reduction in the overall well-being of community members.
